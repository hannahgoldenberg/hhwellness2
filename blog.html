Introducing HairHelper: The First Hair Tie Designed for Women with Shoulder Mobility Limitations

For most people, putting their hair up takes seconds. For women living with frozen shoulder, recovering from a stroke, or navigating life after shoulder surgery, the act of raising their arms above their head to style their hair can be painful, exhausting, or simply impossible. HairHelper was built for them.

Developed by H&H Wellness — a sibling-founded femtech company — HairHelper is a patent-pending adaptive hair tie that functions as a hybrid between a scrunchie and a headband. Instead of requiring the wearer to reach overhead and loop a tie through their hair multiple times, HairHelper slides on over the head like a headband, is moved to the crown, and secured with a single press-and-pull motion. No high reaches. No repetitive overhead movements. No need for assistance.

Women's Health April 2026

What Is Frozen Shoulder and Why Do Women Get It More?

Frozen shoulder — clinically known as adhesive capsulitis — affects up to 5% of the general population, but women are disproportionately impacted. Understanding why, and what daily life looks like for those affected, is the foundation behind HairHelper.

Frozen shoulder develops in three stages — freezing, frozen, and thawing — and can last anywhere from one to three years. During the frozen stage, overhead arm movement becomes severely restricted or entirely impossible, making everyday tasks like styling your hair, reaching for a shelf, or getting dressed a painful challenge.

Stroke Recovery April 2026

Hair Care After a Stroke: The Daily Living Gap Nobody Talks About

Stroke rehabilitation programs cover speech, walking, and gross motor recovery — but the granular, daily-living challenges that women face are rarely addressed. For women who experience hemiplegia or reduced upper limb mobility following a stroke, hair care becomes an unexpectedly significant obstacle.

Occupational therapists often recommend adaptive tools for dressing, eating, and bathing — but adaptive hair accessories have historically been an afterthought. HairHelper is designed as a genuine ADL (Activities of Daily Living) aid: minimal dexterity required, one-handed compatible, and engineered to be used without assistance.

Design Process March 2026

How We Designed a Hair Tie for Minimal Arm Elevation — The Engineering Behind HairHelper

Most hair ties require the user to reach above their head, thread the tie through their hair, and loop it multiple times. Each of those motions requires significant shoulder range of motion — typically 150°+ of abduction. HairHelper was engineered to reduce that requirement dramatically.

The hybrid headband-scrunchie form factor allows the user to place the device over the head at face level, then use natural forward neck tilt to position it toward the crown. The clasp-and-cord tightening mechanism is operated at the back of the crown — still elevated, but far below the range required for traditional styling.
